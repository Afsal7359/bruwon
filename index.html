Our obsession

We chase the
perfect crack.

It starts with a sound — the dry snap of chocolate giving way to a soft, green centre.

Bruwon began in a small kitchen with one stubborn question: could a chocolate bar hold the warmth of fresh kunafa and the richness of pistachio without losing its snap? We toast every strand by hand, fold it through a pistachio cream we make from scratch, and seal it inside a thin shell of single-origin chocolate.

No shortcuts, no fillers, no pretending. Just the most honest version of a flavour the world fell in love with — made slowly, in batches small enough to taste every one.

How it's made.

Four steps, no rushing. This is the order it actually happens in the kitchen — slow toasting, hand-folding, and a final temper that gives every bar its clean snap.

In the kitchen
Tempered by hand
01

Toast

Kunafa strands hit the trays and toast low and slow until they're deep gold and crisp through.

02

Fold

We grind pistachios into cream and fold the cooled kunafa through it for crunch in every bite.

03

Enrobe

Tempered chocolate is cast into moulds, filled with the core, and capped with a thin glossy shell.

04

Set

Bars cool until they snap clean, then they're wrapped, boxed and sent out cold the same week.
